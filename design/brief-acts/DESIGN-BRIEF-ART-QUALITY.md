# ART QUALITY DIRECTIVE — READ BEFORE DRAWING ANYTHING
### Overrides any conflicting note in other briefs. This is what "good" looks like for this saga.

## 1 · FLOWING LINES, NOT ASSEMBLED SHAPES
No composite-of-primitives art. Every character, artifact and world element must be drawn with **continuous, confident, flowing artistic linework** — the ink outline is a living stroke with taper and swell (thick on shadow side, thin on light side), curves that follow anatomy and gesture, never a stack of circles/rects with an outline slapped on. Silhouette test: every character must be recognizable from its filled silhouette alone. If a shape reads as "two ellipses and a rounded rectangle," redraw it.

## 2 · CHARACTERS: BIG, WITH PRESENCE
- Characters are the stars of every screen they appear on. Dialogue portraits render at **minimum 30–40% of screen height**; story tableaux give the featured character **half the frame**; bosses LOOM (Vex, Void, Gnash, the Ten Heads fill 50–70% of the frame in their scenes).
- Presence = gesture + eyes: every pose has a clear line of action (a single sweeping curve through the body), asymmetry, weight. Eyes are large, expressive, catch-lit. No stiff, front-facing, symmetrical standing poses except deliberate comedy.
- In-game player sprites stay readable-small, but their PORTRAIT and TABLEAU versions are full illustrations with interior detail: fur/chitin texture suggestion, fabric folds, wing iridescence — depth, not flat fills.

## 3 · DEPTH IN EVERYTHING
- **Worlds:** every backdrop is a 3-plane painting minimum (far atmosphere / mid forms / near frame elements), with atmospheric perspective (cooler+lighter far, warmer+darker near), a clear light source, and cast shadows that ground objects. Foreground framing elements (a leaning flower stem, a curtain edge, a girder) pull the eye in.
- **Artifacts:** rendered like treasured objects — material identity (wax feels waxy, brass gleams, paper folds crisply), a bounce-light, a contact shadow, and one bespoke detail that rewards a close look (engraving on the Ring Token, honey bubbles inside the Quill).
- **Grey states** keep the depth: desaturation, not flattening — the linework and lighting survive; only the joy drains.

## 4 · ONE SAGA, FIFTEEN DESIGN DIALECTS
Every world keeps the shared foundation (flowing ink line, sticker gloss, big-eyed characters) but speaks its own visual dialect — motifs, line temperament, texture:
- **Meadow/Hive** — round, honeyed, dripping curves; hexagon motifs; soft pollen light.
- **Stage** — art-deco marquee arcs, spotlight cones, velvet folds, brass trim.
- **Cosmos** — long elegant orbits, star-point sparkles, deep gradients, thin glowing lines.
- **Dojo** — brushstroke energy: lines like sumi-e ink, angular speed, paper-screen geometry.
- **Lab** — bouncy glass curves, bubble clusters, coil spirals, neon liquid glow.
- **Critter** — plump, wobbly, hand-drawn warmth; leaf and ripple motifs.
- **Arcade** — chunky pixel-bevels ONLY as surface decoration on flowing forms; neon rim-light, scanline shimmer.
- **Origami** — crisp straight creases meeting soft paper bows; visible fold shadows; flat planes tilting in space.
- **Vibe** — rubbery bounce, waveform ribbons, gradient pops, sticker-on-sticker layering.
- **Dino** — cracked-earth textures, big friendly bulk, fern silhouettes, egg-speckle patterns.
- **Enchanted** — bioluminescent curls, drifting spores, filigree tendrils, moonlit rim.
- **Wildhearts** — windswept strokes, migratory arcs, feather/petal drift, golden-hour warmth.
- **Elements** — four palettes braided; weather energy (swirl, crackle, drip, gust) in the linework itself.
- **Legends** — monumental scale cues, carved-stone weight, mist bands, ancient gold inlay.
- **Turbo** — hot-rod flame curves, motion smears, chrome highlights, checkered accents.
- **Villain spaces** (Carnival, Engine, Grey Sea) — the dialect is THEFT: beautiful shapes with the color stolen; too-perfect symmetry; machine teeth behind pretty curtains.

## 5 · ACCEPTANCE BAR (per delivered asset)
1. Silhouette reads. 2. Line of action visible. 3. Light source consistent + one bounce light. 4. World dialect identifiable at a glance. 5. No untextured flat fill larger than ~15% of the asset. 6. Characters in scenes ≥ the size specified in §2. 7. Grey variant keeps all depth. Assets failing the bar get redrawn, not patched.
